FITabulous - a UCD project

The Project: Fitabulous is a grad school project for my Human Factors Design class. I collaborated on this project with my course partner - Sonali Mattias.

Design Process

1. Research

  1. Target Audience: For easy access and to also better empathize, we chose university students just like us as our target audience.
  2. Method: Interviews
  3. Inquiry: Since 'Healthy living' means different things to different people, we went on an investigation spree and gathered data based on the following factors:
  • What does healthy living mean to you?
  • Do you have healthy living goals
  • How do you seek to achieve these goals?
  • How would you rate yourself in the pursuit of these goals?
  • What factors hinder you from achieving these goals

2. Analyzing Research

Most of our participants thought of 'healthy living' in terms of Diet and Exercise, and something they should do often but do not just have enough time or budget for. These results shaped our design thinking, and so we focused on Diet and Exercise with the sole goal of helping people stay fit.

3. Use Case Scenarios

To better help with our design thinking and decision, we used Use Case Scenario to help understand the user's goals for each problem we aimed to solve and what the system's response should be.

4. Test

Since this is a human factors project, the idea is to test every decision at every stage of design. So we tested with users if our thinking of the use case is actually right and adjusted accordingly

5. User Journey

From the result of the test, we were able to better understand the goals of the users and what their journey from start to completion might look like.

6. Then We Sketch!

Now that we understand how a user might go from one point to the other, we sketched some solutions using paper, markers and stick it notes.

7. Test Again!

Testing the sketches with the users, we were able to understand the missing pieces of our design and improve on them.

8. Prototyping

With the result of the last test, we came up with a prototype and yeah, we tested again but this time focusing on usability problems - ease of use, and if goal was achieved without any assistance.
