Siegwerk Track & Trace

Designing an online track and trace process for bulk ink shipments

Summary

Siegwerk is a 150 year old company that recently started the process of digitalisation for their customers around the world. One of such solutions is the Siegwerk Customer Portal. The portal is used by large companies who do commercialised printing (e.g printing houses who print for brands such as Cocacola) to order inks in bulk. However they still relied heavily on customer support to know when each shipment would arrive or leave the plant.

Hence the question: how might we make the decades long tradition of delivering ink blind to our customers more transparent? To answer this question, it involved understanding how ink production and distribution works, peculiarities to country to country management and distribution of ink, established processes for ink management within Instaff and cross cultural management with account managers across continents to get this feature designed and implemented.

Role

Sole UX / UI designer

The Challenge

How might we make the decades long tradition of delivering ink blind to our customers more transparent?

Project period

March 2019 - June 2019

Process

To answer the question of how to make this process digitally transparent, it involved understanding how ink production and distribution works, peculiarities to country to country management and distribution of ink, established processes for ink management within Instaff and cross cultural management with account managers across continents to get this feature designed and implemented.

  1. Discovery:
    • Multiple sessions with product owner to understand business requirements and perspective
    • Workshop with China customer representatives to understand user needs
    • Workshop with SAP team to understand how tracking data were stored regionally and across the globe for Siegwerk customers
  2. Ideation: Sketching possible solutions and testing with customer representatives, who in turn test with customers in their regions.
  3. Iterating sketches based on customer feedback and technical/business constraints
  4. Hi-fi design and prototyping using the Figma design tool
  5. Feedback loop: The duration spent in explaining design solutions to stakeholders, customer reps and developers to collect feedback and iterate
  6. Final iteration and hand off to developers

    7. Outcome

    The following results were achieved due to the previously mentioned process:

    • We decided to split tracking methodologies for customers due to technical constraints. Customers in regions where logistics provider were available get real time tracking of their deliveries. While customers in other regions get delivery statuses such as “In transit”“ or “delayed”.
    • For china, an emphasis on mobile accuracy was made because majority of customers access the site via Wechat.
    • These features are live and continued to be used till date
